I am an incoming PhD student in the [Department of Political Science at Washington University in St. Louis](https://polisci.wustl.edu/). I am interested in government intervention in social media in the MENA region. Methodologically, I am interested in studying how researchers can use large language models in a reproducible and statistically robust manner in the social sciences.

Prior to joining WashU, I was a Research Associate at [J-PAL MENA](https://mena.jpal.net/), where I worked on the implementation and analysis of randomized controlled trials (RCTs). I earned my B.A. in Political Science from [Cairo University](http://www.feps.edu.eg/enn/) in 2025. During my undergraduate studies, I was a [Sawiris Scholar](https://college.uchicago.edu/people/mohamed-sabry-amer) at the University of Chicago (2024–2025), focusing on data science and empirical social science, and completed a virtual exchange in machine learning through the [MO:VE](https://www.uni-marburg.de/en/studying/incomings/move) program at the University of Marburg.
